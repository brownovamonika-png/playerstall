Like PlayerStall wood lockers, Hockey players are known for their toughness, but they also understand the importance of maintaining their equipment to protect them while on the ice. Keeping your hockey equipment in top shape is crucial for your safety and performance. Here are some tips on how to maintain your skates, helmet, gloves, and apparel:

Skates:

- Make sure they fit properly, as small skates can distract you from the game.- Sharp blades are safer than dull ones, so sharpen them as soon as they start to dull.- Clean blades after every game.- Check the rivets and have them adjusted by a professional if necessary.- Pull out sweaty insoles to dry them out in your wood locker to reduce bacteria.

Helmet:

- Regularly check for dents and cracks, as they can weaken the outer shell and pose a safety hazard.

Gloves:

- Don't put them near a heat source because they can shrink and crack.- Hang them up to dry after every game and use a [homemade sanitizing spray](https://homesteadandchill.com/homemade-hand-sanitizer/) to freshen them up.

Jerseys:

- Wash them thoroughly after every game.- Use a sports locker to hang them up and get them dry, and keep your equipment organized.
