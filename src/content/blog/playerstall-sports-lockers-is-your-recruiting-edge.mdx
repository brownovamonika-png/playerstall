In today's athletic world, there is a growing competition to produce the best facilities. Teams from all levels of competition, including Division 2, Division 3, junior colleges, and club teams, are all building new facilities and lockers are a small yet necessary and integral part of that puzzle. Professional teams, high schools, prep schools, and youth club teams are all looking to outdo their rivals on and off the field. Improvements can include everything from athletic wood lockers and turf fields to locker rooms, practice facilities, training centers, lounges, workout and weight rooms, and even waterfalls. If teams are not making these improvements, student athletes and potential free agents will sign elsewhere.

Athletic team [locker rooms](https://playerstall.com/gallery/) are a critical stop on the recruiting visit and are the selling point to any recruit. The idea is to have players spend more time together in the locker room, and old, dingy metal lockers just won't cut it. With other schools upgrading, don't be the last team to invest in new sports lockers. Investing in new sports lockers is not only good for the team, but it is also a great way to show alumni that their old team is still dedicated to excellence. When former players see the work, dedication, and investment being put into their old team, they will want to help in any way they can. Engaged, active alumni are more willing to give back to athletics, and new wooden lockers can add that personal touch. There is no better way to honor alumni than using donor plaques inside the wood lockers for future players to remember.
