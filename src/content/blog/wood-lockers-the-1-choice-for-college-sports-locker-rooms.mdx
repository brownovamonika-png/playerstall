## What Makes Wood Lockers So Popular?

Coaches who upgrade almost always say the same thing, they wish they had done it sooner. Here is what drives that reaction.

### 1. They Look Incredible

Rich wood grain gives your locker room a high-end, custom feel that metal simply cannot replicate. Your players feel like pros the moment they walk in, and recruits or visiting scouts immediately notice your commitment to quality and detail.

There is a reason the most-photographed locker rooms in college athletics tend to feature custom wood. The visual warmth and craftsmanship communicate something that no metal shelf or painted steel door can: that this program invests in its people.

### 2. They Are Built to Last

Wood lockers are engineered to handle the punishment of daily athletic use. Gear bags, helmet impacts, damp equipment, and years of hard training cycles. High-quality plywood construction resists warping and cracking far better than MDF or particle board alternatives, and with proper care, a well-built wood locker will outlast two or three generations of metal lockers.

### 3. Fully Customizable to Your Sport

Need extra-wide stalls for football shoulder pads and helmets? Want integrated hockey stick racks and skate hooks built right into the unit? Need a dedicated section for lacrosse sticks or basketball sneaker storage? With custom wood lockers, every detail can be tailored to your sport and your facility layout.

Metal lockers come off a shelf. Wood lockers come from a conversation about what your team actually needs. That difference shows.

When players walk into a locker room with beautiful custom wood lockers, it sets a tone. It signals that the program takes them seriously. It creates a sense of belonging and identity, a home base that fuels confidence and team spirit in a way that a row of metal boxes never could.

## Sport-Specific Locker Design for College Programs

One of the clearest advantages of custom wood is the ability to design for your specific sport. College programs have very different storage needs depending on the game, and a one-size-fits-all metal locker rarely fits any sport particularly well.

**Football:** Extra-wide stalls with dedicated helmet shelves, shoulder pad hooks, and under-bench boot storage. Many programs also add custom lock boxes and ventilated compartments to keep gear fresh and valuables secure.

**Hockey:** Stick racks built into the unit, skate hooks at the right height, ventilation slots to dry wet gear between sessions, and easy-clean surfaces to handle the mess that comes with ice sports.

**Basketball:** Tall, clean stalls with visible nameplates, sneaker storage shelving, and a premium look that plays well on social media and in facility tour videos.

**Soccer and Lacrosse:** Flexible mid-size stalls with boot trays and stick/equipment hooks tailored to the specific gear load of each sport.
