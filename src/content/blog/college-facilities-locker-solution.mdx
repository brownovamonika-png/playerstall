## Multi-Sport Considerations

Most college facilities serve multiple sports. This creates unique planning challenges that require intentional design from the start.

### Different Equipment Needs

Each sport has different equipment requirements:

- **Football:** Helmets, shoulder pads, cleats, and multiple uniforms require tall, deep lockers with dedicated helmet shelving and separate shoe storage
- **Basketball:** Shoes, uniforms, and minimal equipment; narrower footprints are often workable for non-revenue shared rooms
- **Hockey:** Skates, sticks, pads, and heavy protective gear demand deeper lockers and integrated stick storage
- **Baseball:** Bats, gloves, cleats, and multiple uniforms benefit from divided lower compartments and bat hooks

Our custom wood lockers can be configured for each sport's specific needs. Designing for sport-specific requirements from the start prevents costly retrofits down the line—and prevents the frustration of lockers that don't actually fit the equipment athletes use every day.

### Shared vs. Dedicated Spaces

Decide whether sports share locker rooms or have dedicated spaces:

- **Shared spaces:** Need flexible, adaptable lockers that work across different equipment profiles
- **Dedicated spaces:** Can be fully optimized for a single sport's requirements and identity
- **Hybrid approach:** Revenue sports get dedicated rooms; non-revenue sports share a configurable multi-use space

For shared facilities, modular wood locker systems allow athletic departments to reconfigure shelving, hooks, and hanging depth between seasons—so a space used for football in the fall can be adapted for lacrosse in the spring without a full renovation.

## Budget Planning for College Facilities

College athletic budgets are often constrained. Here's how to maximize long-term value within realistic capital limits.

### Total Cost of Ownership

Don't just look at upfront costs. Consider:

- 15-year total cost (see our [ROI analysis](/blog/true-cost-sports-lockers-roi))
- Ongoing maintenance costs and annual repair budgets
- Replacement cycles and the operational downtime they create
- Impact on recruiting and the scholarship outcomes tied to facility quality

Metal lockers may carry a lower sticker price, but their total cost of ownership over 15 years—including repainting, hinge replacements, rust treatment, and full replacement cycles—often exceeds the investment in quality wood lockers backed by a five year guarantee. When you model the cost per locker per year, wood consistently wins.

### Tier Selection Strategy

Our five tiers allow you to match budget to specific program needs:

- **Semi Pro ($349):** Great for non-revenue sports and secondary locker rooms
- **Varsity ($449):** Most popular across college programs; strong balance of quality and value
- **Pro ($549):** For revenue sports and recruiting showcase rooms
- **Stadium ($599):** Elite programs and flagship facilities that compete for top national recruits

A practical strategy for multi-sport departments: allocate Stadium or Pro tier for revenue sports like football and basketball—where recruiting ROI is highest—while using Varsity or Semi Pro for non-revenue programs. This delivers a premium experience where it counts most without overextending a limited budget across every space.

### Capital Planning and Phasing

Many college athletic departments phase locker room upgrades across two or three budget cycles. We regularly work with ADs to create a phased roadmap: upgrade the football locker room in year one, basketball in year two, and shared Olympic sports spaces in year three. Phased planning makes large-scale projects manageable and politically easier to approve, without sacrificing quality at any stage.

## Planning for Growth

College programs grow and change. Build in flexibility from the start:

- **Roster size changes:** Modular expansion panels let you add lockers as rosters grow without restarting the design
- **New sports:** Flexible design accommodates the addition of new programs without triggering a full renovation
- **Facility expansion:** Modular systems allow additions that match existing units in finish, dimension, and style
- **Program upgrades:** Easily add updated nameplates, refreshed finishes, or upgraded hardware over time

Building in a 10–15% expansion buffer when initially designing the space costs almost nothing upfront and saves significantly if your roster grows or the university adds a program within the warranty period. Most programs we work with wish they had planned for more space—not less.

## Compliance and Standards

College facilities must meet a range of standards that go beyond basic construction requirements:

- **ADA compliance:** Per <a href="https://www.ada.gov/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">ADA.gov guidelines</a>, accessible locker spaces must meet specific reach range, clear floor space, and hardware requirements. At least 5% of lockers should be ADA-compliant in most multi-use athletic facilities
- **Safety standards:** Proper ventilation, anti-microbial surfaces, and structural anchoring all factor into locker room safety ratings and institutional insurance requirements
- **NCAA guidelines:** The <a href="https://www.ncaa.org/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">NCAA</a> publishes facility standards for member institutions; our design team reviews drawings against those specifications before fabrication begins
- **Institutional standards:** Fire egress, building code compliance, and campus-specific security requirements vary by institution and must be confirmed during the planning phase

Our custom design process includes a compliance review phase where we cross-check drawings against NCAA guidance, ADA dimensions, and local building codes before a single panel is cut. This prevents costly revisions during installation and ensures the finished facility passes inspection the first time.

## Maintenance and Longevity

College facilities need lockers that can handle serious daily use across many years:

- **High use:** Multiple teams using the same space every day, season after season, for years at a time
- **Budget constraints:** Long-lasting solutions reduce annual maintenance budgets and avoid unplanned capital requests
- **Modular design:** Individual panels, shelves, or hardware components can be replaced without a full unit overhaul
- **15+ year life:** Properly maintained wood lockers outlast metal alternatives—no rust, no peeling paint, no denting from normal use

Wood is also easier to refinish than metal. A light sand-and-seal restores the original finish at a fraction of the cost of repainting or replacing metal units. Our five year guarantee backs the structural integrity of every locker we build, giving facilities teams the confidence that the investment is protected.

## College Locker Room FAQ

### How much should a college program budget for new custom lockers?

Most Division I and II programs earmark $400–$700 per locker depending on finishes, integrated power, and branding. We recommend planning across multiple tiers so non-revenue sports can leverage Semi Pro or Varsity lines while football or basketball invests in Pro or Stadium.

### What timeline should athletic directors expect for locker room renovations?

A full design-to-install timeline typically spans 14–20 weeks: 2–4 weeks for discovery and approvals, 6–8 weeks for fabrication, and 4–6 weeks for shipping, install, and punch-list. Start conversations early if you need completion before fall camp or postseason play.

### How do we keep multi-sport locker rooms compliant with NCAA and ADA standards?

Build at least 5% of lockers with ADA-compliant dimensions, integrate anti-microbial ventilation, and coordinate with campus facilities to confirm egress, lighting, and security specs. Partnering with a custom shop ensures drawings are reviewed against NCAA guidance before fabrication begins.

### Can wood locker rooms handle the heavy daily use typical of college programs?

Yes. Our wood locker systems are engineered for high-traffic institutional use. The modular design means any panel, shelf, or hinge can be individually replaced without swapping out the full unit—critical for programs with dozens of athletes using the same space daily across multiple seasons.

### What's the difference between wood and metal lockers for college athletic facilities?

Wood lockers offer a warmer, more professional look that resonates with recruits and alumni. They don't rust or dent, and they're far easier to refinish. Metal lockers carry a lower upfront cost but typically require replacement within 8–10 years due to corrosion and structural wear.
