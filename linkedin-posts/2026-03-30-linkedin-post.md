**Here's something nobody talks about when buying sports lockers: your locker room might be making your players sick.**

I've been building custom wood sports lockers for over 30 years, and this is one of the first things I tell every athletic director who calls me.

Metal lockers trap moisture. Full stop.

A hockey player comes off the ice. A football player peels off pads after a two-hour practice. All that sweat, heat, and humidity has to go somewhere. In a metal locker, it stays. It lingers. It builds up. And within a few seasons, you've got a mold and odor problem that no amount of cleaning spray will fix.

Wood breathes differently.

Solid wood is naturally porous — it absorbs and releases moisture as the environment changes. That natural ventilation cycle means less standing moisture, less bacterial growth, and a locker room that actually smells like a professional facility instead of a gym bag.

I've walked into locker rooms 15 and 20 years after we installed our lockers and they still look great. No rust. No warping. No chronic odor issues. That's not marketing — that's just what happens when you build with the right material.

The honest truth? Most programs don't even factor this in when they're comparing prices. They look at the sticker cost and stop there. But when you're replacing metal lockers every 8–10 years — and dealing with odor complaints and maintenance headaches in between — the "cheaper" option gets expensive fast.

If you're planning a locker room upgrade, I'd encourage you to ask your supplier one simple question: *What happens to moisture in this locker?*

The answer will tell you everything.
