Most people have never thought about what actually goes into building their locker room.

Let me walk you through what happens from the moment we get your order to the day your players walk in and see their name on a locker.

It starts with a conversation — not a form, not an automated quote, a real conversation. I want to know your sport, your team colors, how many players, and what you're trying to accomplish. Are you trying to upgrade a crumbling old facility? Build something that makes recruits stop in their tracks? I need to understand *your* vision before a single piece of wood gets cut.

From there, our team gets to work in our Canadian workshop. Every locker starts as solid hardwood — not particle board, not laminate, not the stuff that warps and rusts after a few seasons. We cut, sand, assemble, and finish each unit by hand. Custom team colors are applied with precision. Logos are added with care. Then everything goes through inspection before it's packed and shipped.

Here's what I find meaningful about that process: when a coach or athletic director opens those boxes, they're not getting something that came off an assembly line in another country. They're getting something built specifically for them, by a family that's been doing this for 30 years and stands behind every single piece.

The lifetime guarantee isn't a marketing line. It's possible because we build things right from the start.
