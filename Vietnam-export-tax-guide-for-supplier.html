Vietnam Tax Guide: Exporting Wood Lockers to Canada

Reference for your team — VAT, export duty, and documentation when exporting wood lockers from Vietnam to Canada.

1. Do you have to pay Vietnamese tax when exporting to Canada?

When you export wood lockers made in Vietnam to Canada, the main Vietnamese taxes to consider are VAT and export duty.

2. VAT (Value Added Tax)

Short answer: No. You do not pay Vietnamese VAT on the export.

Vietnam applies a 0% VAT rate to goods exported to foreign countries when:

  • Goods are delivered and received outside Vietnam.
  • Your company has sufficient documents proving that the goods were delivered and received outside Vietnam (e.g. export customs clearance, transport documents, proof of delivery in Canada).

If these conditions are met and documentation is kept, no Vietnamese VAT is due on the export sale to Canada. If the conditions are not met or documentation is missing, the standard VAT rate (e.g. 10%) may apply.

3. Export duty (export tax)

Vietnam has an export tariff schedule. Some goods are subject to export tax when they leave Vietnam.

  • Whether wood lockers are subject to export tax depends on their HS code and the current Vietnam Export Tariff Schedule (e.g. under wood/furniture chapters).
  • The exact rate (0% or higher) is in the official Vietnam Export Tariff Schedule (updated annually; 2024 and 2025 versions exist).

Action for your team: Check the official Export Tariff Schedule for the HS code of your wood lockers (often Chapter 94 – furniture, or Chapter 44 – wood) to confirm whether any export duty applies and at what rate.

4. Other Vietnamese taxes

  • Corporate income tax (CIT): Export sales are part of taxable profit in Vietnam. This is normal CIT on profit, not a separate “export tax.”
  • Withholding tax: Usually relevant for payments into Vietnam (e.g. from the Canadian buyer), not for the act of exporting the goods.

5. Summary table

Tax type Do you pay it when exporting wood lockers to Canada?
VAT No — 0% VAT on exports when properly documented.
Export duty Maybe — only if wood lockers are listed in Vietnam’s Export Tariff Schedule with a rate > 0%. Check the official schedule.
Corporate income tax (CIT) Export revenue is included in taxable profit (normal CIT rules).

VAT on export — direct answer:

When you export the wood lockers from Vietnam to Canada, that supply is treated as an export of goods. Exports are subject to 0% VAT in Vietnam. So you do not pay Vietnamese VAT on that export, provided you have the usual export documentation (e.g. contract, commercial invoice, export customs declaration, proof that the goods left Vietnam and were received in Canada).

Any VAT/GST due when the goods enter Canada is Canadian tax, not Vietnamese.

6. Recommended steps for your team

  1. Check the Vietnam Export Tariff Schedule (e.g. 2024/2025) for the HS code of your wood lockers (e.g. Chapter 94 – furniture, or Chapter 44 – wood) and confirm whether an export duty applies and at what rate.
  2. Keep full export documentation: contract, invoice, packing list, export customs declaration, bill of lading, and proof of delivery in Canada — so you can correctly apply 0% VAT on the export.
  3. For a definitive answer on export duty and CIT, consult the General Department of Vietnam Customs or a Vietnam customs/tax advisor.
