# Container Capacity Calculator

## Input Parameters

**Container (40-foot standard):**
- Internal Length: 39.5 feet
- Internal Width: 7.75 feet  
- Internal Height: 7.833 feet

**Wood Sheets:**
- Dimensions: 4 feet × 8 feet
- Thickness: 3/4 inch (0.0625 feet)
- Sheets per lift: 50

## Calculation

### Per Layer Capacity
- **Lengthwise:** 39.5 ft ÷ 8 ft = 4.9375 → **4 sheets**
- **Widthwise:** 7.75 ft ÷ 4 ft = 1.9375 → **1 sheet**
- **Sheets per layer:** 4 × 1 = **4 sheets**

### Vertical Stacking
- **Height per lift:** 50 sheets × 0.0625 ft = **3.125 feet**
- **Container height:** 7.833 feet
- **Lifts that fit:** 7.833 ÷ 3.125 = **2.506 → 2 full lifts**

### Total Capacity
- **Option 1 (Standard stacking):** 2 lifts × 50 sheets = **100 sheets total**
- **Option 2 (If stacking lifts on top):** 2 lifts × 4 sheets/layer × 50 sheets = **400 sheets** (theoretical maximum if sheets are stacked individually)

## Answer

**You can fit 2 full lifts (100 sheets total) in a 40-foot standard container.**

### Breakdown:
- **Lifts:** 2 lifts
- **Total sheets:** 100 sheets
- **Total volume used:** ~6.25 feet height (2 × 3.125 ft)
- **Remaining height:** ~1.58 feet (unused space)

## Notes

- This assumes sheets are stacked flat (4 ft × 8 ft orientation)
- Some space may be wasted due to container dimensions not perfectly matching sheet dimensions
- Consider weight limits: 40-foot container max weight ~58,800 lbs
- Each sheet weight should be calculated to ensure you don't exceed weight capacity
